Automating Molecular Biology Procedures Using Liquid-Handling Robots


January 2024 - May 2024

Principle Investigator: Dr. Catalin Voiniciuc
Institution: University of Florida
Department: Horticultural Sciences


Research Focus:

This research project aimed to make molecular biology experiments faster and more accurate by using robots to do the work that scientists usually have to do by hand. These robots, called Opentrons OT-2, can handle many liquid samples at once with extreme accuracy, which is very helpful for scientists who need to test a lot of samples. By programming Python protocols for these robots, I simplified and scaled up tasks like DNA assemly and plasmid extraction. This not only saves time and effort but also helps scientists find new discoveries more quickly and with fewer errors.


Responsibilities:

I was responsible for programming the protocols in Python that my PI and other students in the lab needed.
